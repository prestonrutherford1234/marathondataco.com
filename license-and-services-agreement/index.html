License and Services Agreement

Last updated: October 3, 2023

This License and Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between Marathon Data, LLC, a Delaware limited liability company ("Marathon"), and the customer ("Customer") accessing and using Marathon's services.

1. Services

1.1 Description and Nature

Customers may access the Brand Health Dashboard and additional services Marathon provides, which can be modified at Marathon's discretion.

1.2 Right to Use

Marathon grants a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable right to use services for internal business purposes during the agreement term.

1.3 Feedback

Customers agree to cooperate in providing feedback and identifying errors or defects.

1.4 Ownership

Marathon retains sole ownership of services and all intellectual property rights, including patents, copyrights, and trade secrets.

1.5 Use Restrictions

Customers cannot reverse engineer, sublicense, or use services for competitive analysis. Only authorized employees or contractors may access the platform.

2. Customer Materials and Data

Customers retain ownership of their uploaded information and data. Marathon may use, display, and modify customer materials to provide services and develop deidentified insights.

3. Representations and Warranties

Both parties represent they have authority to enter this agreement. Customers warrant that Marathon's use of their materials won't violate laws or infringe third-party intellectual property rights.

4. Term

4.1 Trial Period

Customers receive free access to certain platform aspects during an initial trial period.

4.2 Subsequent Agreement

Upon trial expiration, customers must negotiate in good faith for a paid subscription.

5. Termination

Either party may terminate with 30 days' written notice. Marathon may terminate immediately for breach of use restrictions or IP infringement. Upon termination, customers must stop using services, return confidential information, and pay outstanding amounts.

6. Payment Terms

Customers agree to pay mutually agreed fees monthly in arrears using Marathon's specified payment method.

7. Confidentiality

Neither party discloses the other's confidential information without need-to-know basis or legal requirement. Marathon implements appropriate security measures protecting personal data.

8. Disclaimer and Limitation of Liability

8.1 Disclaimer

The services are provided "as is." Marathon makes no warranty or representation regarding the services and disclaims all warranties.

8.2 Exclusion of Damages

Neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages, except for breaches of confidentiality, use restrictions, or IP infringement.

8.3 Liability Cap

Liability is limited to fees paid in the preceding six months or $100, whichever is greater.

9. Indemnification

Marathon defends customers against IP infringement claims. Customers defend Marathon against claims arising from their materials or service use.

10. Miscellaneous

  • Assignment requires written consent except in reorganization or change-of-control.
  • Governed by Delaware law.
  • Complete agreement between parties.
  • No partnership or agency relationship created.

11. Data Processing Addendum

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") forms part of the Agreement and governs the processing of personal data by Marathon on behalf of the Customer.

11.1 Processing Terms

Marathon processes personal data only as necessary to provide the services described in this Agreement, in accordance with the Customer's documented instructions. Marathon shall not process personal data for any purpose other than as set forth herein without the prior written consent of the Customer.

11.2 California-Specific Terms (CCPA)

To the extent the California Consumer Privacy Act ("CCPA") applies, Marathon acts as a "Service Provider" as defined under the CCPA. Marathon shall not sell personal information, retain, use, or disclose personal information for any purpose other than performing the services specified in this Agreement, or as otherwise permitted by the CCPA.

11.3 Service Optimization

Marathon may use personal data in an aggregated and deidentified form to improve and optimize the services, provided such use does not identify any individual Customer or data subject. Any insights derived from such data shall be the property of Marathon.

11.4 Aggregation

Marathon may aggregate Customer data with data from other customers to create anonymized, statistical datasets. Such aggregated data shall not contain any personally identifiable information and may be used by Marathon for benchmarking, analytics, and service improvement purposes.

11.5 Information Security and Incidents

Marathon implements and maintains appropriate technical and organizational security measures designed to protect personal data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. In the event of a confirmed personal data breach, Marathon shall notify the Customer without undue delay and in no event later than 72 hours after becoming aware of the breach, providing sufficient information to enable the Customer to meet any obligations to report the breach to relevant authorities or data subjects.

11.6 Cross-Border Transfers

To the extent personal data is transferred outside the Customer's jurisdiction, Marathon ensures that appropriate safeguards are in place, including Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission or other transfer mechanisms recognized under applicable data protection laws. Marathon shall inform the Customer of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of sub-processors involved in cross-border transfers.

11.7 Audits and Data Deletion

Marathon shall make available to the Customer all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Customer or an auditor mandated by the Customer. Upon termination of the Agreement or upon the Customer's written request, Marathon shall delete or return all personal data processed on behalf of the Customer, unless retention is required by applicable law.

11.8 Customer Obligations

The Customer represents and warrants that it has obtained all necessary consents and authorizations required under applicable data protection laws for the processing of personal data by Marathon. The Customer is responsible for ensuring the accuracy, quality, and legality of the personal data provided to Marathon and the means by which such data was acquired.

11.9 Processing Details Summary

  • Subject matter: Provision of the services described in this Agreement
  • Duration: For the term of the Agreement
  • Nature and purpose: Processing customer data to provide analytics, reporting, and growth optimization services
  • Types of personal data: Names, email addresses, phone numbers, addresses, payment details, and service usage patterns
  • Categories of data subjects: Customer's end users, employees, and business contacts

11.10 Supplemental Standard Contractual Clauses Terms

Where the Standard Contractual Clauses ("SCCs") apply, the following supplemental terms govern: Marathon acts as the data importer and the Customer acts as the data exporter. The parties agree that the SCCs are incorporated by reference into this DPA and shall be deemed completed with the information set forth in this Agreement. In the event of any conflict between this DPA and the SCCs, the SCCs shall prevail to the extent of such conflict. Marathon shall promptly inform the Customer if, in its opinion, an instruction from the Customer infringes applicable data protection legislation.
